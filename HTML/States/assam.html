Introduction

Assam, the gateway to Northeast India, is a land of lush tea gardens, mighty rivers, and vibrant traditions. Known for its silk, temples, and wildlife, Assam represents a perfect blend of cultural heritage and natural beauty. The capital city is Disput. Guwahati is the most important city of the state.

Art & Crafts

Assam is globally renowned for its handloom industry, especially the production of Muga silk, Eri silk, and Assam silk. Traditional bamboo and cane crafts also showcase the artisans’ remarkable skills.

Festivals

Bihu is the most celebrated festival of Assam, marking the changing seasons and agricultural cycles. Rongali Bihu, celebrated in April, is especially vibrant with dance, music, and feasts. Other important festivals include Ambubachi Mela at Kamakhya Temple and Majuli Raas.

Cuisine

Assamese cuisine is simple yet flavorful, with rice and fish as staples. Famous dishes include Khar, Masor Tenga (sour fish curry), and Duck curry. Assam tea is world-famous, cherished for its strong and malty flavor.

Monuments & Heritage

Assam boasts ancient temples like Kamakhya and Sivasagar’s Ahom monuments, which reflect its glorious past. The Majuli island, world’s largest river island, is also a cultural hub of Neo-Vaishnavite monasteries (Satras).

Dance

Sattriya is a classical Indian dance form from Assam, founded in the 15th century by Sankaradeva to promote the Vaishnava faith. Preserved in the Sattras (monasteries), it's known for its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive hand gestures (hastamudras), all governed by strict principles. The dance, which involves singing, dancing, and music with instruments like the khol and flute, tells stories from Hindu epics and was traditionally performed by monks but is now performed by both men and women.

Dress

Assamese culture traditional dress is Mekhela Chador for Female, Dhoti and Gamosa for Male.
