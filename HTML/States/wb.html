Introduction

West Bengal, located in eastern India, is a vibrant state known for its literature, music, and cultural richness. Kolkata, its capital, is called the "City of Joy" and has been a hub of art, education, and intellectual movements. From the Himalayas in the north to the Sundarbans in the south, Bengal is diverse in geography and heritage.

Art & Crafts

West Bengal is famous for its terracotta art of Bishnupur, Shantiniketan’s handicrafts, and the traditional Kantha embroidery. Clay idols of Goddess Durga crafted in Kumartuli, Kolkata, are admired worldwide for their detail and artistry.

Festivals

Durga Puja is the grandest festival of West Bengal, marked by artistic pandals, idols, music, and celebrations. Other important festivals include Kali Puja, Saraswati Puja, and Poila Boishakh (Bengali New Year).

Cuisine

Bengali cuisine is known for its love of fish, rice, and sweets. Famous dishes include Macher Jhol (fish curry), Shorshe Ilish (hilsa in mustard), Mishti Doi, Sandesh, and the world-famous Rasgulla. Street food like puchka (pani puri) is also immensely popular.

Monuments & Heritage

West Bengal has a glorious heritage reflected in monuments like the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, Dakshineswar Kali Temple, and the terracotta temples of Bishnupur. The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger.

Dance

Bengal has a rich tradition of folk dances like Chhau, Gambhira, and Baul songs. Rabindra Nritya, inspired by Rabindranath Tagore’s works, blends storytelling with graceful movements and is an integral part of Bengal’s cultural identity.

Dress

The traditional attire of Bengali women is the white saree with a red border, especially during Durga Puja. Men often wear dhoti-kurta. Bengal cotton and Baluchari sarees are globally famous for their elegance and craftsmanship.
